The Ryvite Story

It started with a birthday party. Rylan was turning 1, and his dad needed an invite. How hard could it be?

Turns out, $8 per invite on Paperless Post hard. Or "pick from 200 of the same template" hard. So instead of just making the invite, dad opened Claude, described the vibe, uploaded a few inspiration photos, and got back a fully custom, beautifully designed HTML invitation in about 90 seconds. It looked better than anything on the premium platforms. And it was free.

That should have been the end of it. Make the invite. Send the invite. Eat the cake. But instead, the thought crept in: "What if everyone could do this?" And then the completely over-engineered fun project began — auth systems, RSVP tracking, AI pipelines, database schemas — all because a 1-year-old needed a birthday party invite.

Ryvite is free for now because the whole point is that beautiful invitations shouldn't cost $8 a pop. If you love what we're building, donations go to Rylan's college fund — so that someday he can look back and know his first birthday kicked off something real.

Forbidden Words

Avoid these when describing Ryvite:

CHEAP • BASIC • TEMPLATE • GENERIC • SIMPLE (negative) • DISRUPTING • STARTUP JARGON

These words diminish the value and heart behind what Ryvite is. We're not a workaround or a pitch deck — we're a family project that makes something people love.

🎓 Donation Messaging Guidelines

Tone: Warm, self-aware, a little funny. This whole thing started because a dad over-engineered a birthday invite. Lean into that energy. The donation is optional and framed as supporting the kid whose birthday started it all.

Placement: After the user has already gotten value (post-invite creation, in the footer, on the about page). Never before or during the creation flow.

Example CTAs: "Love your invite? Rylan's 1st birthday started all this — donations go to his college fund. 🎓" or "This whole platform exists because of a birthday party. Help fund the kid's future."

Never say: "Pay what you want," "Support us," or anything that sounds like a paywall is coming.

Guidelines

  • Minimum Font Size: 16px for body text, 14px for small text
  • Line Height: Minimum 1.5 for readability
  • Button Size: Minimum 44px for touch targets
  • Color Alone: Never use color alone to convey meaning; add icons or text labels
  • Alt Text: All images must have descriptive alt text
  • Focus States: All interactive elements must have visible focus indicators

Motion & Animation

Subtle, celebratory, and purposeful. Motion should feel like joy.

Entrance Animations

Curve: cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1) (bounce out)

Duration: 0.6s–0.8s

Use: When elements first appear on screen

Hover States

Curve: ease-out

Duration: 0.3s

Use: Buttons, links, cards should scale or glow

Interactions

Curve: cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)

Duration: 0.2s–0.4s

Use: Form inputs, toggles, modals

Celebrate Moments

Curve: ease-out

Duration: 0.8s–1.2s

Use: RSVP submitted, invite sent, milestone moments

Principles

  • Keep it light: Avoid rapid, jarring animations
  • Purpose-driven: Every animation should communicate or delight
  • Respectful: Provide a prefers-reduced-motion option
  • Consistent: Reuse the same easing curves across the product
